## Purpose

This file is the pricing foundation for The AI Plug.

It is not a public pricing sheet yet.

It exists to stop pricing drift and stop the business from being priced around vague AI package names.

The rule going forward:

**Price real deliverables first. Package names come second. AI language comes third.**

A roofer, restaurant owner, attorney, transportation operator, or boutique owner should hear the service in their own words first.

Examples:

Bad:
> AI Business Command System

Better:
> Website, Google Ads landing page, quote/photo request form, follow-up templates, owner lead tracker, and monthly reporting for roofing leads.

Bad:
> AI Customer Path Fix

Better:
> A better way to collect calls, quote requests, photos, scheduling details, and estimate follow-up so fewer opportunities get lost.

This file should be read before updating:
- dashboard pricing
- proposal builders
- sales scripts
- training manuals
- one-sheets
- website business-services copy
- vertical playbooks
- restaurant/home-services/professional-services pricing

# Master Pricing Rule

## Do not price by AI labels

Do not start with:
- AI Growth System
- AI Advantage Package
- Business Command System
- Customer Path System
- Lead Leak Fix

Start with:
1. What does the client actually want?
2. What does the client call the problem?
3. What real deliverables are included?
4. What tools are needed?
5. How much setup/build work is required?
6. What needs ongoing management?
7. What risk/approval is involved?
8. What should be custom quoted?

Then package it.

Packages are built from modules.

Use when the client has one clear issue.

# Dashboard Pricing Rules

The dashboard can show internal pricing guidance, but it must not make pricing look automatically approved.

Every pricing area should include:

> Internal pricing guidance only. Confirm scope, owner approval, pass-through costs, launch requirements, and monthly support before quoting.

Do not generate:
- invoices
- payment links
- final quote totals
- deposit requests
- customer-facing price promises

Copy buttons can draft pricing explanation, but should make clear that final pricing requires owner approval.
