## 2. Core pricing philosophy

### The AI Plug does not sell random tools

The AI Plug sells the customer path behind the business.

That path may include:

1. attention
2. website / landing page
3. customer questions
4. smart intake
5. routing
6. follow-up
7. AI-assisted answers
8. internal workflow
9. owner visibility
10. monthly improvement

### Price the connected system, not the visible page

A simple-looking page can be cheap or expensive depending on what it connects to.

A page with no intake and no routing is one thing.

A page connected to smart intake, alerts, follow-up, AI answer planning, reporting, content rhythm, and owner-visible workflows is a business system.

### Start with the smallest useful system

The safest first sale is usually not a giant full system.

The best first offer is the smallest system that fixes a real business leak.

### Use pricing ranges, not fake precision

Early offers should use pricing ranges because the final scope depends on:

- number of pages
- number of forms
- number of routing destinations
- number of AI answer topics
- number of approvals needed
- content volume
- industry guardrails
- automation complexity
- monthly management needs

## 9. Proposal pricing language

### Use this language

The price depends on how many pieces need to connect, how many approvals are required, and how much ongoing management you want.

A simple page is one thing. A page connected to intake, routing, follow-up, AI answers, content, and owner visibility is a business system.

The safest first step is to identify the first useful system, build that, test the workflow, and expand only if it makes sense.

### Avoid this language

- “This will get you more leads.”
- “This will pay for itself.”
- “You will rank higher.”
- “You will get more bookings.”
- “The AI will handle everything.”
- “You will not need staff.”
- “We guarantee results.”
