## Purpose

This file resets The AI Plug direction back to real business-owner language, real services, real deliverables, and real pricing logic.

This file overrides older dashboard/manual language when that older language sounds like:
- vague AI packages
- agency framework language
- "customer path" as the first explanation
- "lead leak" as the first explanation
- "AI advantage" without plain business deliverables
- pricing that was created before the full service inventory was understood

The goal is simple:

**Client language first. Real service deliverables second. AI/internal system language third.**

The AI Plug is not just an AI agency.

The AI Plug is a modern digital marketing, website, paid ads, lead system, automation, dashboard, and AI business systems company built around real owner problems.

## Master rule

Never lead with abstract AI language when talking to a business owner.

Bad first explanation:
> We build AI-powered customer path systems.

Better first explanation:
> For a roofing company, we can help with better roofing calls, quote requests, photo intake, estimate follow-up, Google Ads landing pages, and a simple owner dashboard so you know who needs a callback. AI helps us build, write, organize, and improve parts of that faster.

Internal language is allowed inside the dashboard and operations, but client-facing language must sound like the business owner's world.

# Pricing Foundation Rules

## Price by deliverable, not package name

Do not price vague AI labels first.

Start with:
1. What does the client need?
2. What real work will be delivered?
3. What tools are required?
4. What setup/build time is required?
5. What risk/approval is involved?
6. What monthly work continues?

Only then assign the package name.

## One-time vs monthly

### One-time setup/build
- websites
- landing pages
- forms
- dashboards
- automations
- assistants
- setup
- audits
- SOPs
- tracking setup

### Monthly
- ad management
- content/social management
- SEO/content
- dashboard/workflow support
- AI assistant updates
- reporting
- optimization
- campaign management
- monthly recap
- ongoing AI opportunity review

## Pass-through costs

Always separate:
- ad spend
- software subscriptions
- phone/voice usage
- SMS usage
- email usage
- premium tools
- domains/hosting where applicable
- third-party platform fees

## No guarantee rule

Never guarantee:
- leads
- calls
- jobs
- bookings
- reservations
- traffic
- rankings
- revenue
- reviews
- QR scans
- ad ROI
- appointment volume
- AI search placement
