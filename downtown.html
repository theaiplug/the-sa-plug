Activity guide

Things to do in Downtown San Antonio

Downtown works best when you know what kind of stop you want first: history, River Walk scenery, family activities, indoor escapes, cultural stops, or event-night movement. Use this guide to pick the right downtown move before you waste the night bouncing between random stops.

Best first-time downtown move

Start with the Alamo, River Walk, and Tower of the Americas if it is your first time. Add Market Square or La Villita when you want more culture and color without turning the day into a long drive.

Best family downtown move

Start with River Walk boat energy, then use Rivercenter, LEGOLAND, SEA LIFE, LiggettVille, Magik Theatre, or Hemisfair when kids need indoor time, shade, or space to reset.

Best convention downtown move

Keep the plan walkable. River Walk, Buckhorn, Hopscotch, Briscoe, San Fernando Cathedral, and nearby drinks work better between sessions than chasing far-apart stops.

Best resort guest downtown move

If you are coming in from a resort, make downtown worth the ride. Pick one main stop, one dinner area, and one after-dinner walk before you leave the resort zone.

Historic Icons + Cultural Landmarks

The Alamo

Google: 4.6 ★ · 150K reviews
18th-Century FortressTexas HeritageFree Timed Entry

Historic Landmark · Downtown / Alamo Plaza · Free admission; timed entry for church sanctuary

The historic soul of Texas and the most important first-time downtown stop. Best when visitors want the classic San Antonio history moment without needing a long museum day.

Best for: first-time visitors, history enthusiasts, international visitors, families wanting an educational walk.

Insider tip: The grounds are free, but reserve timed entry online if you want to step inside the church sanctuary.

San Fernando Cathedral

Google: 4.8 ★ · 4.2K reviews
Oldest Cathedral in TexasVideo ArtFree Admission

Historic Landmark · Downtown / Main Plaza · Free admission

A beautiful historic cathedral and one of the best downtown night stops when “San Antonio | The Saga” projection show is running.

Best for: architecture lovers, couples, casual night walks, visitors who want a memorable free downtown moment.

Insider tip: Check the current schedule for “San Antonio | The Saga,” the free projection show on the cathedral facade.

Google: 4.6 ★ · 4.5K reviews

A classic Texas saloon-and-museum stop with historic bar energy, wildlife displays, Texas Ranger history, and a strong “old San Antonio” feel.

Best for: families with kids who like wildlife exhibits, history buffs, visitors who want a drink in a historic space.

Insider tip: Tied to the historic Rough Riders story and works well as a quirky downtown add-on.

Dinner + night-out

Downtown dining + nightlife moves

Downtown works best when you know the kind of night you want first: barbecue, steakhouse, River Walk dinner, Mexican culture, rooftop drinks, historic bars, or a quieter lounge. Use this guide to pick the right dinner and night-out move before the group starts bouncing between random stops.

Best downtown dinner move

Choose Bohanan’s when the night needs polish, Paesanos when River Walk dinner utility matters, Biga when the group wants elevated food by the water, and Domingo when modern Mexican and patio energy fit the night.

Best casual downtown food move

Choose Pinkerton’s for Texas barbecue, Schilo’s for historic lunch, Mi Tierra for Market Square culture, and Casa Rio for classic River Walk Tex-Mex on the iconic umbrella patios.

Best rooftop move

Choose Aleteo or The Moon’s Daughter for upscale skyline energy, Fairmount Rooftop when seafood and cocktails matter, and Rosario’s Rooftop when the group wants louder Southtown night-out energy.

Best after-dinner drink move

Use Esquire when you want historic bar energy, Downstairs at The Esquire when you want a darker hidden lounge, and Bar 414 when blues history and classic cocktails sound better than a crowded patio.
